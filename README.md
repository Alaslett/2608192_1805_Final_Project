# 1805_Final_Project

https://alaslett.github.io/2608192_1805_Final_Project/ 

This is a collaborative project which explores our relationship to technology and the instant gratification we seek from it. This project takes on stylistic qualities similar to games such as Terraria, StardewValley and aesthetically relates back to early games such as Mario. The rationale behind this decision was to transport the user back in time to simple technology in a time where technology bought us childhood wonder and allowed us to slow down from the fast pace of life. This is explained by the appearance of the DS on the levels forcing the user to interact with dated technology to remind the user of their childhood in a time where technology seemed much more of a novelty than an aspect of everyday life. We wanted the game to be slightly difficult in our levels with the first level being an invisible maze, the second falling phones and the third a snake game. This was deliberate as we wanted it to represent the grasp technology has on us and how hard it is to escape from it. The levels are also difficult to create a sense of frustration for the user so that the gratification of completing the game can be delayed, which is important as happiness is soo easy to get through technology nowadays and its important to remember that we need to stay present with all the technological temptations around us. 

The Sprite design was inspired by pixel art with the character being similar to Robin from StarDew valley, a game Amy likes to play as it becomes an escape from a busy urban life. The DS was chosen to represent nostalgia as it was our first interaction with technology and creates a nice contrast with the high tech we are presented nowadays. Sprite design was done by Amy.

Backgrounds were done by Mari and it was important to differentiate between styles as the background becomes more simplistic throughout the game to allude to the individual returning to their childhood as represented by the background on the final screen. 

Level 1 is a maze as it represents the difficulty to escape from technology feeling that you're stuck in a loop with the grasp it has on you. Level 2 has a simple collision mechanic with falling phones to represent the temptation of technology even when we escape from it. Level 3 is inspired by the classic Snake Game which was seen in many different ways in early technology which creates a sense of nostalgia for the player at the end. 

-- CHALLENGES -- CODING THOUGHTS --

The game was a big challenge for both of us to make as we have little coding experience which meant we turned to examples from Moodle, p5.js and Youtube to give us a foundation to start on which we could then make our own. We also took advice from Tom and Mathew which was really insightful for us as we were able to identify the issues and learn where we were going wrong and ways in which we could simplify what we were doing. Overall we are very happy with how the game turned out as it took a lot of trial and error to make especially because we were working on different devices and had little communication at times. One of the biggest challenges was definately putting our codes together as some of our movement mechanics and values were different which meant we had to adapt different variables so the game could continue to work. We both contributed to each level, helping each other with errors and communicating our improvements to each other as well as adapting what we already had to fit within the idea and what we were trying to achieve. 

Furthermore, we decided the tileMap was the best way of establishing collisions and movement as it was very difficult to make them work without this as well as the screen transitions which took a lot of time to figure out. Moodle really helped us to get back to the basics and give us a starting point of our game for what we could make our own using our own knowledge as well as help from p5.js and Youtube. We chose to follow the Coding Trains video on the snake game to help us with the final level so that we could make it fun and interpret it as our own. This was a challenge as we had to interpret the variables to fit our game as well as use a seperate movement mechanic as 9 times our of 10 the sprite would not correctly move or interact with the objects. It was a bit frustrating to do and with most things in this game took a lot trial and error but was soo satisfying when it was complete. 

We have learnt soo many different things about code in this project and it has been really exciting to see how the game develops over time and how code can be interpreted in different ways depending on how each individual codes. It expressed that code is such an art form and one that needs to be respected because there are soo many ways to tell the computer to do something. It has made us enthused about what we can achieve in our upcoming coding projects and how our styles will evolve, if anything it has taught us to have more patience with code which fits well with our theme as a game about instant gratification has taught us how awarding it can be when you work towards something and put the work in to achieve what we sought out to achieve and enjoy the process.
